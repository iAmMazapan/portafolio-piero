About Me

I am a Computer Science student at Universidad Nacional de San Antonio Abad del Cusco (UNSAAC), graduating in 2025. My passion lies in competitive programming, where I thrive on solving complex algorithmic challenges and pushing the boundaries of efficient problem-solving.

As an active member of the ACM Cusco Study Circle and Omega Up Club, I collaborate with talented peers to explore advanced algorithms and data structures. I have had the privilege of representing my university at multiple ICPC Latin America Regional Programming Contests (2022-2024), competing against the best programmers in the region.

Recent Activities

In 2024, I participated in the Training Camp Argentina in Rosario, an intensive program where I trained alongside top competitive programmers from across Latin America. This experience significantly enhanced my problem-solving skills and exposed me to cutting-edge algorithmic techniques.

I was honored to receive first place at NASA Space Apps Challenge 2024 in Cusco with my team's project "Farm Guardians". We created an educational game that uses real NASA satellite data to help farmers make informed decisions about their crops, bridging the gap between space technology and agriculture.

Teaching & Mentorship

Beyond competing, I am passionate about sharing knowledge. I mentor junior students through programming workshops, helping them develop strong foundations in algorithms and problem-solving. I believe in fostering a collaborative learning environment where students can grow together and support each other's journey in competitive programming.
